About Alzheimer's

Alzheimer's is a disease that affects memory, thinking, and behavior.

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects memory, thinking skills, and ultimately, the ability to carry out simple tasks. It is the most common cause of dementia among older adults, but it can also affect individuals in their midlife. Alzheimer's progressively worsens over time, and currently, there is no cure. However, with proper understanding and care, individuals with Alzheimer's can live meaningful lives.

Reasons For Dementia

Reason 1

Genetic Factors

Certain genetic mutations and variations can predispose individuals to Alzheimer's, although it's not the sole determinant, familial history plays a crucial role.

Reason 2

Age

As individuals grow older, the risk of developing Alzheimer's disease increases significantly, with most cases occurring after the age of 65.

Reason 3

Head Trauma

A history of severe head injuries, particularly those resulting in loss of consciousness, has been associated with an increased risk of developing Alzheimer's disease later in life.
